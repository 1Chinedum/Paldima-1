Our Services.

Porcelain Tiles Collection.

Porcelain tiles are a type of ceramic tile made from refined clay and fired at extremely high temperatures, which makes them dense, durable, and less porous than standard ceramic tiles. Because of their strength and resistance to moisture, they’re ideal for both indoor and outdoor use—on floors, walls, and even countertops. They come in a wide variety of styles, including designs that mimic natural stone or wood, making them a popular choice for modern interiors. Thanks to their low water absorption rate, they’re especially well-suited for bathrooms and kitchens.

Outdoor Matt Tiles Collection.

Outdoor matte tiles are specially designed for exterior spaces like patios, walkways, balconies, and pool surrounds. Their non-glossy, textured surface provides excellent slip resistance, making them a safe and stylish choice for areas exposed to rain or moisture. They’re typically made from durable materials like porcelain or vitrified ceramic, which can withstand harsh weather conditions and heavy foot traffic. Plus, their muted finish helps hide dirt and scuffs better than glossy tiles—so they stay looking fresh with less fuss.

Ceramic Tiles Collections.

Ceramic tiles are a popular and versatile building material made from natural clay that’s shaped, glazed, and fired in a kiln. They’re known for being affordable, easy to maintain, and available in a wide range of colors and patterns, making them a go-to choice for both walls and floors in homes and commercial spaces. They’re best suited for indoor use, especially in kitchens, bathrooms, and living areas. While not as dense or water-resistant as porcelain, ceramic tiles still offer good durability and are easier to cut and install—perfect for DIY projects or budget-conscious renovations.

                                                    Accordion FAQ.
  • What type of finishes are available for tiles?
    Tiles come in polished, matte, satin, glossy, and textured finishes, each offering different aesthetics and functional benefits.
  • How do i ensure my tiles last longer?
    Proper installation, using the right adhesive, sealing grout lines, and regular cleaning with non-abrasive cleaners help extend tile lifespan.
  • Are ceramic tiles durable?
    Yes, ceramic tiles are durable, but they are slightly less resistant to wear and moisture compared to porcelain tiles. Proper installation and maintenance enhance their longevity
  • Are ceramics tiles best-suitable for outdoor projects?
    Ceramic tiles are generally durable, but they must be rated for outdoor use to withstand weather conditions.
  • Are ceramics tiles best suitable outdoor use?
    Yes, but only certain types. Look for tiles labeled as frost-resistant, slip-resistant, and low-porosity—porcelain tiles usually check all those boxes.
